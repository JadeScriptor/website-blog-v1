About Gundam

Write by Adrian Fahren Setiawan

Gundam is a popular Japanese media franchise created by Sunrise, featuring giant robots called "Mobile Suits," with the titular "Gundam" being the most iconic. Launched in 1979 with the anime "Mobile Suit Gundam," it revolutionized the mecha genre by introducing realistic, militaristic themes and complex, human-driven stories. The franchise has expanded into multiple TV series, films, manga, novels, and model kits known as Gunpla. It explores war, politics, and humanity's future, resonating with audiences worldwide. Gundam's influence extends to popular culture, technology, and even space exploration, cementing its legacy as a cornerstone of science fiction.

The Gundam universe is vast, with multiple timelines and alternate realities, such as the Universal Century, After Colony, and Cosmic Era. Key themes include the horrors of war, the ethics of advanced technology, and the struggle for peace. Iconic Mobile Suits, like the RX-78-2 Gundam and Wing Gundam, have become cultural icons. Gundam's influence extends to popular culture, technology, and even space exploration, cementing its legacy as a cornerstone of science fiction. The franchise continues to innovate, bringing new stories and advanced Gunpla models to fans old and new.
